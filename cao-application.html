Complete CAO Application Guide

Important Update

Use your March 2025 Results for your application. Applications for 2026 academic year are now open. General closing dates are between 30 September and 30 October (varies by course).

Step 1: Visit the CAO Website

Start by visiting the official Central Applications Office website:

  • Open your browser (Google Chrome, Safari, etc.)
  • Type www.cao.ac.za in your address bar
  • You only need a small amount of data to access the site (approximately 5-10MB)

Direct Link: www.cao.ac.za

Step 2: Start Your Application

On the CAO homepage:

  • Click on the "Apply" button at the top of the page
  • You'll be directed to the application portal
  • If you're a new applicant, click on "Continue with Application"
  • If you already have a CAO number, click on "Login" and enter your existing credentials

Step 3: Preparing Your Personal Information

Before you begin, make sure you have the following information ready:

Required Personal Details

Personal Details

  • Full Name (as it appears on your ID)
  • South African ID Number
  • Gender
  • Date of Birth
  • Race (for statistical purposes)
  • Home Language
  • Marital Status
  • Email Address (active account you check regularly)
  • Phone Number
  • Alternative Phone Number (if available)
  • Physical Address (with postal code)

Next of Kin / Guardian Details

  • Full Name of parent or guardian
  • Relationship (Father, Mother, Guardian, etc.)
  • ID Number
  • Contact Phone Number
  • Email Address (if available)

Academic Details

  • Name of your current/last high school
  • List of subjects you're currently studying
  • Latest academic results (March 2025 results)
  • Previous year's final results (where applicable)

Important Privacy Notice

When sharing information with us, protect your personal data. Never share your full ID number or other sensitive information online. Only send us your EasyPay number once you've completed your application.

Step 4: Selecting Your Courses & Universities

The CAO system allows you to select up to 6 program choices across KwaZulu-Natal institutions:

  • Choose wisely: Research programs that align with your academic strengths and career goals
  • Diversity your choices: Include a mix of competitive and less competitive programs
  • Consider multiple institutions: Apply to several universities to increase your chances

Application Strategy

When selecting your programs, include a mix of "reach," "match," and "safety" options:

  • 1-2 Reach Programs: Competitive programs that might be challenging to get into
  • 2-3 Match Programs: Programs where your marks align with typical admission requirements
  • 1-2 Safety Programs: Programs where your marks exceed the typical requirements

Step 5: Uploading Your Documents

The CAO requires certain documents to verify your application. Make sure they are clear and legible.

Required Documents

  • Certified copy of your ID document (must be certified within the last 3 months)
  • Grade 11 final report or latest available report
  • Grade 12 March 2025 results (or your most recent academic results)
  • Power of Attorney form (download from the CAO website, print, sign, and upload)

How to Upload Documents:

  1. Scan or take clear photos of your documents using your phone
  2. Make sure all text is readable and the entire document is visible
  3. On your CAO profile, click "Upload Documents"
  4. Select the document type, browse for your file, and upload
  5. Verify the document appears in your profile

Document Photo Tips

If you don't have access to a scanner, follow these tips for taking clear document photos with your phone:

  • Find good lighting (natural daylight works best)
  • Place documents on a flat, dark surface
  • Hold your phone directly above the document (not at an angle)
  • Make sure the entire document fits in the frame
  • Check that all text is sharp and readable before uploading

Step 6: Getting Your EasyPay Number & Payment

After completing your application, you'll receive an EasyPay number. This is the only information you need to send to us for sponsorship.

How to Get Your EasyPay Number

  1. Complete all sections of your application
  2. Submit your application
  3. The system will generate an EasyPay number (format: 10-digit number)
  4. Take a screenshot or write down this number

How to Send Us Your EasyPay Number:

Send only the following information to us via WhatsApp (064 878 4287):

Hi, I've completed my CAO application. Here's my EasyPay Number: [Your 10-digit number] My full name is: [Your full name] Thank you.

Important

DO NOT send us your:

  • Full ID number
  • CAO login details
  • Home address
  • Any other personal information

We only need your EasyPay number to make the payment on your behalf.

Step 7: Tracking Your Application

After we've made the payment, you should:

  • Regularly check your email for communications from CAO and universities
  • Log in to your CAO account to monitor the status of your application
  • Upload any additional documents requested by institutions
  • Update your academic results as you receive them (e.g., Trial Exams, Final Matric results)

Next Steps After Applying

Once your application is complete and payment is made:

  • Check your CAO status regularly using the "Check My Application" feature
  • Look for "Waiting for Decision" status beside your program choices
  • Watch for provisional acceptances that may arrive from August onwards
  • Update your profile with new academic results as you receive them
  • Respond promptly to any requests for additional information

Frequently Asked Questions

How many programs can I apply for?

You can apply for up to 6 programs across all CAO member institutions in KwaZulu-Natal with a single administration fee. Choose your programs strategically, including a mix of competitive and less competitive options to maximize your chances.

What if I want to change my program choices after applying?

You can change your program choices after submitting your application using the "Change of Mind" feature on the CAO website. Log in to your CAO account, click on "Change of Mind," and follow the instructions to update your choices. Be aware that some programs may have already closed for applications by the time you make changes.

How do I know if S.E.E has paid my application fee?

After you send us your EasyPay number, we will process the payment within 48 hours. You can verify that payment has been received by logging into your CAO account and checking your application status. You should see "Payment Received" or similar confirmation. We will also send you a confirmation message once payment has been made.

What documents do I need to upload for my CAO application?

You need to upload the following documents:

  • Certified copy of your ID (certification must be within the last 3 months)
  • Grade 11 final report
  • Grade 12 March 2025 results (or your most recent academic results)
  • Signed Power of Attorney form (download from the CAO website)

If you're an international applicant, you'll also need to upload a copy of your passport and study permit (if applicable).

How will I know if I've been accepted?

Universities will communicate their decisions through the CAO system. You should:

  • Regularly check your email (the one you provided during application)
  • Log in to your CAO account to see application statuses
  • Look for "Provisionally Accepted" or "Accepted" statuses

Institutions may start sending out provisional acceptances from August onwards, with final acceptances coming after Matric results are released in January.

What if my March results aren't very good?

It's best to apply with your current results, even if they aren't as strong as you'd like. You can update your CAO profile with improved results later (June exams, September trials, final Matric). Universities will consider your latest available results when making admission decisions.

Also, consider including some programs with lower entrance requirements among your choices to increase your chances of acceptance.

Can I apply after the closing dates?

Yes, for most programs you can still apply after the closing date, but your application will be marked as "Late Application" and will only be considered after all on-time applications have been processed. Also, from November 1st, a higher "Late Payment" administration fee will apply.

However, some competitive programs (like Medicine) explicitly state "No Late Applications will be considered" - for these programs, applications received after the closing date will not be considered at all.

Why does S.E.E require me to do my own application?

We require students to complete their own applications for several important reasons:

  • Security: Your personal information remains under your control
  • Direct communication: You receive all updates and information directly from CAO and universities
  • Skill development: You learn valuable application skills that will benefit you in the future
  • Responsibility: You take ownership of your educational journey

Our goal is not just to provide financial support but to empower you to navigate these processes independently.

What happens if I'm not one of the 5 selected students for sponsorship?

Even if you weren't selected for sponsorship, this guide is still valuable for you! You can follow all the steps to complete your CAO application independently. The standard CAO application fee (for on-time applications) is typically R250-R300, which you would need to pay yourself through methods like EFT, bank deposit, or retail payment partners like Shoprite/Checkers or Pep.
