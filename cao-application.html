CAO Application Guide for 2026 Entry

Welcome to our comprehensive guide for applying to KwaZulu-Natal universities through the Central Applications Office (CAO). Applications typically open in 2025 for 2026 academic year.

Complete CAO Application Guide

Disclaimer: Information and dates provided in this guide are for general guidance based on typical CAO cycles. Always refer to the official CAO website (www.cao.ac.za) for the most current and accurate information, including deadlines and fees for the 2026 application cycle.

Important Update for 2026 Entry

Typically, you will use your Grade 12 March/June results from 2025 (or latest available Grade 11/12 results) for your initial application. Applications for the 2026 academic year generally open around May 2025. General closing dates vary by course and institution, often between late August and late October 2025.

Step 1: Visit the CAO Website

Start by visiting the official Central Applications Office website:

  • Open your web browser (e.g., Google Chrome, Safari, Firefox).
  • Type www.cao.ac.za into the address bar and press Enter.
  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection. Basic access usually requires minimal data.

Official Link: https://www.cao.ac.za

Step 2: Start Your Application

On the CAO homepage:

  • Look for and click on the "Apply" or "Applications" button/link (typically prominent).
  • You'll be directed to the application portal.
  • If you are a new applicant, select an option like "New Application" or "Continue with Application".
  • If you have applied before or already have a CAO number, look for a "Login" option and use your existing credentials.

Step 3: Preparing Your Personal Information

Before you begin filling out the online form, gather the following information:

Required Information Checklist

Personal Details

  • Full Name (as per your ID/Passport)
  • South African ID Number (or Passport number for international applicants)
  • Gender
  • Date of Birth
  • Population Group/Race (often for statistical purposes)
  • Home Language & Other Languages
  • Marital Status
  • A reliable Email Address (you must check this regularly!)
  • Your active Phone Number
  • Alternative Phone Number (if available)
  • Physical Address and Postal Address (with postal codes)
  • Citizenship / Country of Origin

Next of Kin / Guardian Details

  • Full Name of parent or guardian
  • Relationship to you (e.g., Father, Mother, Guardian)
  • Their ID Number (if applicable)
  • Their Contact Phone Number
  • Their Email Address (if available)

Academic Details

  • Name of your current/last high school
  • Examination Authority (e.g., Department of Basic Education, IEB)
  • List of subjects you are studying or have completed
  • Your latest academic results (e.g., Grade 11 final, Grade 12 March/June 2025)
  • Previous year's final results if applicable.
  • NSC / Matriculation examination number (if you have already written finals)

Important Privacy Notice for S.E.E. Sponsorship

If you are applying for S.E.E. sponsorship, only send us your EasyPay number and full name once you've completed your CAO application. Never share your full ID number, CAO login details, or other sensitive personal information via unsecured channels like WhatsApp messages to unverified contacts.

Step 4: Selecting Your Courses & Institutions

The CAO system typically allows you to select up to 6 program choices across various KZN institutions with one application fee.

  • Research Thoroughly: Investigate programs that align with your academic strengths, interests, and career aspirations. Check specific admission requirements for each program.
  • Diversify Choices: Include a mix of programs – some that are highly competitive ("reach"), some where your marks align well ("match"), and some where you comfortably meet requirements ("safety").
  • Consider Multiple Institutions: Applying to programs at different universities can increase your chances of acceptance.

Application Strategy Tip

When selecting your program choices (usually up to 6):

  • 1-2 Reach Programs: Ambitious choices where admission is highly competitive.
  • 2-3 Match Programs: Programs where your academic profile aligns well with typical admission requirements.
  • 1-2 Safety Programs: Programs where your marks likely exceed the minimum requirements, providing a good fallback.

Step 5: Uploading Your Documents

The CAO application requires you to upload certified copies of certain documents. Ensure scans or photos are clear, legible, and complete.

Commonly Required Documents

  • Certified copy of your ID document (South African citizens) or Passport (international applicants). Certification should typically be recent (e.g., within the last 3-6 months).
  • Grade 11 final report/results.
  • Latest Grade 12 results (e.g., March 2025, June 2025, or Trial exam results as they become available).
  • If you have completed Matric: Certified copy of your National Senior Certificate (NSC) or equivalent.
  • Power of Attorney form (Declaration by Applicant): This form is usually downloadable from the CAO website during the application process. It needs to be printed, signed by you (and a parent/guardian if you are a minor), and then uploaded.
  • International applicants may need additional documents like study permits, SAQA evaluations, etc. Check CAO requirements.

How to Upload Documents:

  1. Scan your documents or take clear, well-lit photographs. Ensure the entire document is visible and all text is readable.
  2. Save files in accepted formats (e.g., PDF, JPG). Keep file sizes reasonable.
  3. Log in to your CAO application profile.
  4. Navigate to the "Upload Documents" section.
  5. Select the correct document type for each file and upload it.
  6. Verify that all uploaded documents appear correctly in your profile and are legible.

Document Photo/Scan Tips

  • Use good, even lighting (natural daylight is best, avoid shadows).
  • Place documents on a flat, contrasting surface (e.g., dark table for white paper).
  • Hold your phone or scanner directly above the document to avoid distortion.
  • Ensure the entire document is in the frame, with no cut-off edges.
  • Check for sharpness and readability before saving and uploading. Many phone "Notes" apps have a document scanning feature.

Step 6: Application Fee Payment & EasyPay Number (for S.E.E. Sponsorship)

After completing and submitting your online application form, the CAO system will provide you with payment instructions and a unique reference number (often an EasyPay number if that's a payment option).

If you are not sponsored by S.E.E: You will need to pay the CAO application fee yourself using one of the methods provided by CAO (e.g., EasyPay at a retailer, EFT, credit card online). Follow CAO's instructions carefully.

Step 7: Tracking Your Application & Next Steps

After your application is submitted and the fee is paid (either by you or by S.E.E. if sponsored):

  • Regularly check your email: This is the primary way CAO and institutions will communicate with you.
  • Log in to your CAO account: Periodically check the "My Application" or similar section on the CAO website to monitor your application status for each program choice.
  • Upload updated results: As you receive new academic results (e.g., mid-year, trial, or final Matric results), ensure you upload them to your CAO profile as required.
  • Respond to offers: If you receive an offer of admission, there will be a deadline to accept or decline it. Read all communications carefully.

What to Expect After Applying

  • Your application status might initially show as "Awaiting Documents," "Awaiting Payment," then "Processing," and eventually "Waiting for Decision" for each program.
  • Institutions make decisions at different times. Some may issue provisional offers based on Grade 11/early Grade 12 results, while others wait for final Matric results.
  • Provisional offers may start from August/September onwards, with firm offers typically made after final Matric results are released (usually in January).
  • Be patient, but proactive in checking your status and emails.

Typical Application Timeline for 2026 Entry

Note: These are general timeframes. Always confirm specific dates on the official CAO website.

Around May 2025

Applications Open

CAO applications for the 2026 academic year typically open. Early application is encouraged.

Late August / Early September 2025

First Closing Dates (e.g., Health Sciences)

Closing date for certain high-demand programs like Medicine, Diagnostic Radiography often earlier than others. Check specific program deadlines.

Late September 2025

Major Closing Date (On-time Applications)

General deadline for most undergraduate programs at UKZN, DUT, MUT, UNIZULU to be considered an "on-time" application.

Late October 2025

Final Closing Date (before late fees)

Often the last date for applications for many programs before higher late application fees apply.

From November 2025

Late Applications Period

Late applications may still be accepted for some programs, usually with a higher application fee. Availability is limited.

October - December 2025

Final Matric Exams & Results Submission

Grade 12s write final exams. Ensure your final NSC results are submitted to CAO/institutions as soon as they are available (often automatically done by DBE, but verify).

January 2026

Final Offers & Matric Results Release

NSC Matric results are typically released. Institutions make final firm offers based on these results.

February 2026

Registration & Studies Begin

Successful applicants register at their respective institutions, and the academic year commences.

Frequently Asked Questions

With a single CAO application and fee, you can typically apply for up to 6 program choices. These choices can be across different CAO member institutions in KwaZulu-Natal. It's wise to use all or most of these choices strategically.

CAO usually offers a "Change of Mind" facility, allowing you to amend your program choices online via your CAO profile. There might be a small fee and specific periods when this is allowed. Be aware that if you change to a program whose application deadline has already passed, your change might not be considered. Always check the CAO website for current Change of Mind rules and deadlines.

If you are a S.E.E. sponsored student, after you send us your EasyPay number, we will aim to process the payment promptly (e.g., within 48-72 business hours). You can then verify payment by logging into your CAO account; the application status should update to reflect payment received. S.E.E. may also send you a confirmation message once payment is made.

Commonly required documents include:

  • Certified copy of your ID (or passport for international students).
  • Grade 11 final results.
  • Latest Grade 12 results (March, June, Trials).
  • Completed and signed Power of Attorney/Declaration form (from CAO website).
  • If you've completed Matric, your NSC certificate.

Always check the specific document checklist on the CAO website for the current application cycle, as requirements can vary.

Institutions communicate their decisions primarily through the CAO system and via the email address you provided. You should:

  • Regularly check your email (including spam/junk folders).
  • Log in to your CAO online profile to view the status of each program choice (e.g., "Pending," "Provisional Offer," "Firm Offer," "Unsuccessful").

Provisional offers can come out from August/September onwards, while firm offers are usually made after final Matric results in January.

It's still crucial to apply on time using your current available results. Universities understand that these are not final results. You will have opportunities to upload improved results (e.g., Trial exams, final Matric results) to your CAO profile later. Institutions will typically consider your best/latest results when making final decisions. Applying early secures your application in the system.

For many programs, CAO may accept "late applications" after the main closing dates, often with a higher application fee. However, late applications are typically only considered after all on-time applications have been processed, and space may be limited. Some highly competitive programs (like Medicine) may explicitly state "No Late Applications Accepted." It is always best to apply before the initial closing dates.
